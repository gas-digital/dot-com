The CDR

In Phase 1 a new data warehouse, or CDR, was built on a modern, cloud-based platform with an architecture for storing high-volume, high-velocity and high-variety data. Now in Phase 2, the team will load GCMA, energy efficiency and third-party demographic data while also establishing policies and procedures to expand the usability of the platform to the broader GAS organization.

Analysts will increase usage of the CDR to create and run queries, combine different data sources, and extract the kinds of customer and company insights that were only imaginable before.

Business applications will continue to receive data-driven insights, and Market Strategy will look to partner with additional areas where data is pivotal to creating value and expediting growth.

The Opportunities

The market and regulatory environment are rapidly changing. Now is the time to leverage data and analytics as a strong foundation and business enabler for GAS.

Data and analytics are a corporate asset because they’re more deeply integrated into the ways we work than ever before. When unleashed and fully utilized, data and analytics will generate value across the business in areas like operational excellence, innovation, customer relationships, and risk management.

The CDR is the foundation to help us be more competitive and apply insights and intelligence to every decision, every opportunity, and every outcome.

Capabilities

Included but not limited to

  • Data sharing
  • Anonymization of data for external analysis and use
  • Segmentation, profiling and targeting
  • Propensity modeling for new products and services
  • Large sample billing verification
  • Tariff compliance design
  • Rate design studies and modeling

The current and future uses of the new, cloud-based CDR are vast. It’s gives us the large amounts of data we need, and it’s a tool that lets us work with data itself.

Now, with the first analytics warehouse in the organization, we can examine large and varied data sets to uncover hidden patterns and correlations, market trends, and customer preferences. That data can then be used to inform strategies, monitor customer satisfaction, provide better service, cut costs, and more.

The CDR is the foundation to help us be more competitive and apply insights and intelligence to every decision, every opportunity, and every outcome.
