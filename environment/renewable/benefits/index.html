The Benefits of Renewable Natural Gas

RNG is a sustainable, waste-derived gas produced from naturally occurring methane that emits from landfill, agricultural and food waste sites. When used, RNG is a carbon-neutral form of energy made from the organic waste that every community produces.

Natural gas and its infrastructure are foundational tools that will continue to carry the nation’s energy sources with renewable gas serving as an essential puzzle piece in a cleaner energy future. RNG acts like conventional natural gas in all heating applications in meaning that local utilities can send it directly to your home without the need to upgrade existing appliances or existing natural gas infrastructure. RNG reduces emissions from existing waste streams while also providing a clean, safe, reliable, and affordable fuel. This technology is available and scalable today, and increased use would impact emissions immediately.

Production Technologies

RNG projects capture methane from existing waste streams, such as those at landfills, farms and water resource recovery facilities, and redirect it away from the environment, improving air quality. RNG can also be derived from renewable electricity. In either form, it provides a practical, cost-efficient solution that turns waste into clean energy for homes and businesses.

RNG from Organic Waste

When organic material decomposes, it emits methane. RNG facilities capture this methane and repurpose it, making it into an energy source that looks and acts like natural gas but is carbon-neutral, and in certain circumstances can be considered carbon negative.

Anaerobic digesters or thermal gasification systems are used to process the organic waste into methane-rich biogas, which is then cleaned and conditioned to meet pipeline quality requirements. Using RNG doesn’t require updates to existing gas appliances, equipment or infrastructure, making the transition to this fuel seamless for end-users and energy companies alike.

RNG from Power to Gas

RNG can be produced from power-to-gas processes, in which renewable electricity is used to convert water into renewable hydrogen by using a process called electrolysis. Then, using a methanation process, the hydrogen is converted to methane, which is renewable gas.
