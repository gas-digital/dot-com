Environmental Benefits of Renewable Natural Gas

A diverse portfolio of green energy sources is needed to combat climate change. Similar to wind and solar, Renewable Natural Gas (RNG) is a key part of the solution, but it can’t solve the world’s climate crisis alone – we need to work together.

RNG provides a reliable and storable source of fuel that is compatible with existing infrastructure and can be used by local businesses, homes and vehicles.

Waste is a biproduct of any community, and RNG provides a near-term solution for effectively managing the greenhouse gas emissions from waste, while simultaneously providing an alternative source of green energy.

RNG plays an essential role in the transition to a clean- energy future and in the fight against climate change.

How RNG contributes to the fight against climate change:

Reduced CO2

RNG sourced from landfill diverted food and green waste can provide a 125 percent carbon dioxide reduction, and RNG from dairy manure can result in a 400 percent carbon dioxide reduction when replacing traditional vehicle fuels

Solving Solid Waste

Solid waste is expected to grow nearly 70% by 2050 – RNG is the near-term solution to address this growing problem and convertthe waste for clean, productive everyday use.

Reducing Methane

Methane emissions from manure management rose by two-thirds between 1990 and 2018 to 2.5m tonnes, according to the US Environmental Protection Agency.
